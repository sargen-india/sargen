# SARGEN™ Constitutional Succession

Version: 1.0

---

## Purpose

This document defines how continuity of SARGEN™ shall be preserved across generations, custodians, and leadership transitions.

Succession exists to protect the framework from dependency on any single individual.

The objective of succession is continuity.

Not replacement.

---

## Article 1 — Principle of Continuity

SARGEN™ is designed to outlast its creator, founders, custodians, and participants.

The framework shall continue regardless of changes in leadership, stewardship, or operational responsibility.

No individual shall be considered indispensable to the existence of the system.

---

## Article 2 — Succession of Roles

Roles may change.

Responsibilities may transfer.

Authority may move.

The framework remains.

Succession applies to roles and responsibilities rather than individuals.

No role shall permanently belong to any person.

---

## Article 3 — Qualification for Succession

Future stewards and custodians should demonstrate:

* Understanding of SARGEN principles
* Commitment to continuity
* Respect for governance structures
* Ability to maintain documentation
* Capacity for responsible evolution

Succession shall prioritize capability over status.

---

## Article 4 — Documentation Requirement

No succession process shall rely solely on verbal knowledge.

Critical knowledge, processes, governance records, and operational guidance should be documented and preserved.

Institutional memory must remain accessible beyond any individual.

---

## Article 5 — Continuity During Transition

Leadership transitions should minimize disruption to governance and operations.

The departure, resignation, incapacity, or death of a steward shall not suspend the framework.

The system shall remain operational throughout transition periods.

---

## Article 6 — Founder Transition Principle

The creator of SARGEN™ is the originator of the framework, but not its permanent dependency.

A successful framework is one that continues to function after its founder is no longer active.

The ultimate test of succession is continuity without the founder.

---

## Article 7 — Multi-Generation Stewardship

Each generation of stewards inherits both responsibility and obligation.

Stewards are expected to preserve what works, improve what is necessary, and document what changes.

Every generation acts as a temporary custodian of a longer journey.

---

## Article 8 — Protection Against Succession Failure

Where succession uncertainty exists, preservation of constitutional principles shall take priority over speed of appointment.

Temporary stewardship may be established to maintain continuity until a suitable successor is identified.

Continuity is more important than convenience.

---

## Article 9 — Legacy Responsibility

Succession is not merely the transfer of authority.

It is the transfer of responsibility, knowledge, discipline, and vision.

Every successor inherits the duty to leave the framework stronger than it was received.

---

## Closing Declaration

Individuals are temporary.

Roles are transferable.

Stewardship is inherited.

The framework endures.

The purpose of succession is to ensure that SARGEN™ remains capable of serving future generations while remaining faithful to its foundational principles.
