# SARGEN™ Constitutional Stewardship

Version: 1.0

---

## Purpose

This document defines the responsibilities of individuals entrusted with the stewardship of SARGEN™.

Stewardship exists to protect continuity, integrity, and evolution.

Stewards do not own the framework.

They preserve it.

---

## Article 1 — Stewardship Principle

SARGEN™ is intended to outlast any individual, team, or generation.

Those who operate within the framework act as temporary stewards.

Their responsibility is to protect and strengthen the system for future participants.

---

## Article 2 — Stewardship Over Ownership

Ownership concerns possession.

Stewardship concerns responsibility.

Within SARGEN™, stewardship shall take precedence over personal ownership interests whenever the continuity of the framework is at risk.

A steward serves the system.

The system does not serve the steward.

---

## Article 3 — Preservation Responsibility

Every steward shall work to preserve:

* Constitutional integrity
* Governance standards
* Operational continuity
* Institutional knowledge
* Historical records
* Evolution capability

Actions that weaken these foundations are inconsistent with stewardship.

---

## Article 4 — Duty of Documentation

Stewards shall ensure that important decisions, processes, amendments, and transitions are documented.

Knowledge retained only in memory creates dependency and continuity risk.

Documentation protects future generations.

---

## Article 5 — Duty of Continuity

Stewards shall act to ensure uninterrupted operation of the framework.

The absence, departure, incapacity, or death of any steward shall not prevent continuation of the system.

A successful steward prepares the system to operate without them.

---

## Article 6 — Duty of Evolution

Stewardship is not preservation through stagnation.

Stewards are responsible for enabling responsible evolution while protecting constitutional integrity.

The framework must remain adaptable without losing its identity.

---

## Article 7 — Neutrality of Stewardship

Stewards shall act in the interest of the framework rather than personal advantage.

Personal preference, emotion, status, or influence shall not override documented principles.

Stewardship requires discipline before preference.

---

## Article 8 — Stewardship Transfer

Stewardship may be transferred through documented succession procedures.

A steward may leave.

Stewardship may change hands.

The framework shall remain.

---

## Article 9 — Stewardship Accountability

Stewardship carries responsibility rather than privilege.

A steward is accountable for actions that affect continuity, governance, and evolution.

Authority may grant execution.

Stewardship requires responsibility.

---

## Closing Declaration

Stewards are temporary.

The framework is enduring.

The purpose of stewardship is not control, but preservation, continuity, and responsible evolution.

Every steward serves as a bridge between the past, the present, and the future of SARGEN™.
