# SARGEN Constitutional Amendments

## Purpose

The Constitution exists to preserve the identity, continuity, and stewardship of SARGEN across generations.

Amendments allow adaptation when necessary while protecting the foundational principles upon which SARGEN is established.

The amendment process exists to ensure that evolution never destroys continuity.

---

## Amendment Authority

A constitutional amendment may only be proposed, reviewed, approved, and recorded through the governance framework established by SARGEN.

Recognized Stewards are responsible for administering this process and maintaining constitutional records.

No amendment shall be considered valid unless it is formally recorded within the SARGEN Amendment Register.

---

## Categories of Amendments

### Minor Amendments

Changes that improve clarity, organization, formatting, terminology, or administrative procedures without altering constitutional intent.

Examples include:

* Structural refinements
* Language clarification
* Documentation updates
* Recordkeeping improvements

---

### Major Amendments

Changes affecting:

* Stewardship rules
* Succession principles
* Constitutional authority
* Governance framework
* Long-term continuity mechanisms

Major amendments require formal review, documented justification, and constitutional approval before adoption.

---

## Amendment Principles

Every amendment must satisfy the following conditions:

1. Preserve continuity.
2. Respect founding intent.
3. Strengthen stewardship.
4. Avoid concentration of power.
5. Protect future generations.
6. Maintain constitutional coherence.

---

## Amendment Record

Each approved amendment shall be assigned:

* Amendment Number
* Amendment Title
* Date Adopted
* Steward Responsible
* Reason for Amendment
* Summary of Changes

The official record shall be maintained in the Amendment Register.

---

## Constitutional Integrity

No amendment may invalidate the existence of SARGEN itself.

No amendment may remove the principle of stewardship.

No amendment may eliminate the succession framework without replacing it with an equivalent mechanism that preserves continuity.

No amendment may contradict the foundational principle that systems should operate through governed structures rather than dependence upon individuals.

---

## Living Constitution

The Constitution is intended to endure.

It may evolve through responsible stewardship, but its purpose remains constant:

To preserve SARGEN, its identity, its continuity, and its legacy across generations.

---

## Guiding Principle

**Creation Must Evolve.**

Evolution is permitted through constitutional governance, while continuity remains protected through stewardship and succession.
