# SARGEN™ Constitutional Authority

Version: 1.0

---

## Purpose

This document defines the source, scope, and limits of authority within the SARGEN™ ecosystem.

Authority exists to execute the system.

The system does not exist to serve authority.

---

## Article 1 — Source of Authority

Authority originates from the framework, not from individuals.

No person possesses authority by identity, seniority, ownership, influence, or personal status.

Authority exists only when a role has been formally defined and documented within the system.

Without a documented role, authority does not exist.

---

## Article 2 — Role-Based Authority

Authority is attached to roles, not to people.

Individuals temporarily occupy roles.

Roles may continue beyond the participation of any specific individual.

When a role changes, authority transfers with the role.

Authority never remains attached to a person after the role ends.

---

## Article 3 — Constitutional Hierarchy

Authority operates within the following hierarchy:

Rules

Constitution

Governance

Operations

Roles

Individuals

Every layer derives legitimacy from the layer above it.

No lower layer may override a higher layer.

---

## Article 4 — Limits of Authority

Authority may execute decisions, manage responsibilities, and coordinate operations.

Authority may not suspend constitutional principles.

Authority may not invalidate documented governance.

Authority may not override the foundational rule:

Creation Must Evolve.

---

## Article 5 — Accountability

All authority remains accountable to documented rules, processes, and governance standards.

Undocumented decisions possess no constitutional standing.

Authority without accountability is considered governance failure.

---

## Article 6 — Founder Limitation

The creator, founder, or original custodian of SARGEN™ is subject to the same constitutional structure as all other participants.

No individual receives permanent authority.

The framework remains superior to its creator.

---

## Article 7 — Transfer of Authority

Authority may be delegated, transferred, suspended, or concluded through documented governance procedures.

The continuity of the system shall not depend upon the presence of any individual authority holder.

The role may change.

The authority may transfer.

The system shall continue.

---

## Closing Declaration

Authority is a temporary responsibility granted for execution.

It is not ownership.

It is not privilege.

It is not permanence.

Within SARGEN™, authority serves the framework, and the framework serves evolution.
