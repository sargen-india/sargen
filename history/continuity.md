# SARGEN™ Continuity Framework

Version: 1.0

---

## Purpose

The purpose of continuity is to ensure that a governed system remains operational, understandable, and sustainable beyond the involvement of any single individual.

SARGEN™ is designed with the assumption that people change, organizations evolve, and circumstances shift over time.

The system therefore prioritizes continuity of rules over continuity of personalities.

---

## Continuity Principle

A system should remain functional even when its creators, operators, or beneficiaries are no longer present.

Governance must outlive individuals.

Documentation must outlive memory.

Rules must outlive authority.

---

## Documentation Continuity

All significant governance decisions should be documented.

Core principles, operating rules, and structural definitions should be preserved in durable records.

Future participants should be able to understand system intent through documented evidence rather than verbal interpretation.

---

## Operational Continuity

Processes should be designed so that execution can continue without dependence on a specific person.

Roles may change.

Responsibilities may transfer.

Rules remain.

---

## Knowledge Preservation

Critical knowledge should be stored in documented form whenever possible.

Knowledge concentrated within a single individual creates operational risk.

Preserved knowledge improves resilience and long-term stability.

---

## Governance Continuity

Changes to governance structures should be recorded with clear rationale and historical traceability.

Successive generations should be able to understand:

* What changed
* Why it changed
* When it changed

Governance evolution should remain observable.

---

## Long-Term Perspective

SARGEN™ encourages long-term system thinking.

Decisions should consider future sustainability in addition to present efficiency.

The framework recognizes that durable systems are built through accumulated consistency rather than short-term optimization.

---

## Continuity Objective

The ultimate objective of continuity is preservation of system integrity across time.

Individuals contribute to systems.

Systems should remain capable of operating beyond individuals.
