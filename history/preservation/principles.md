# Core Principles of SARGEN™

## Purpose

This document defines the foundational principles that guide the governance, operation, preservation, and evolution of SARGEN™.

These principles serve as the permanent foundation of the framework and provide continuity across generations, organizations, technologies, and implementations.

## Principle 1 – Governance Before Authority

Authority should operate within governance structures rather than governance being dependent on authority.

## Principle 2 – Rules Before Individuals

Systems should be governed by defined rules and processes rather than individual discretion.

## Principle 3 – Accountability Through Structure

Responsibilities, actions, and outcomes should remain traceable through documented structures and governed execution.

## Principle 4 – Controlled Evolution

Systems should continuously improve while preserving governance integrity and foundational purpose.

## Principle 5 – Long-Term Sustainability

Decisions should consider long-term continuity, maintainability, and resilience beyond immediate requirements.

## Principle 6 – Preservation of History

Historical records should be retained to ensure transparency, learning, and continuity across generations.

## Principle 7 – Creation Must Evolve

The framework recognizes that all creations should evolve responsibly while maintaining structural integrity and original purpose.

## Preservation Rule

Core principles may be clarified, expanded, or interpreted through governance processes, but should not be removed without preserving historical records and documenting the rationale for change.
