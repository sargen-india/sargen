# SARGEN™ Publication Framework

Version: 1.0

Status: Strategic Framework

---

## Purpose

The purpose of this framework is to establish standards governing the creation, review, publication, versioning, maintenance, archival, and retirement of official SARGEN™ publications.

Publication supports continuity by preserving authoritative records, governance decisions, framework evolution, and historical context.

---

## Strategic Objective

The objective of publication is not content production.

The objective is preservation, traceability, continuity, and authoritative communication.

Publications should ensure that:

* Official information remains accessible.
* Framework evolution remains traceable.
* Historical records remain preserved.
* Version history remains verifiable.
* Public references remain authoritative.

---

## Publication Principles

Official publications should be:

* Accurate
* Traceable
* Versioned
* Preserved
* Consistent
* Governed

Publication should strengthen continuity rather than create fragmentation.

---

## Publication Categories

The framework recognizes the following publication categories.

### Foundational Publications

Documents that establish framework principles, governance structures, constitutional authority, and long-term direction.

Examples:

* Whitepapers
* Charter Documents
* Constitutional Documents
* Governance Frameworks

---

### Operational Publications

Documents supporting implementation, interpretation, education, or operational use.

Examples:

* Guides
* Reference Documents
* Standards
* Framework Extensions
* Governance Diagrams

---

### Historical Publications

Documents preserving institutional memory and framework history.

Examples:

* Milestones
* Amendment Registers
* Stewardship Registers
* Historical Archives

---

## Publication Status

Official publications may carry one of the following statuses.

### Draft

Under development and subject to revision.

### Public Release

Approved for public reference.

### Superseded

Replaced by a newer authoritative version.

### Archived

Preserved for historical continuity.

---

## Versioning Principle

Official publications should maintain version identifiers whenever practical.

Examples:

* v1.0
* v1.1
* v2.0

Versioning supports traceability and historical preservation.

---

## Publication Authority

Official publications shall be released through recognized SARGEN™ stewardship processes.

Publication authority remains subordinate to constitutional authority and governance principles.

---

## Official Publication Criteria

A publication may be considered an official SARGEN™ publication when it satisfies the following conditions:

* Consistent with established governance and constitutional principles.
* Published through recognized SARGEN™ stewardship processes.
* Assigned an identifiable title or reference.
* Maintained within an official publication channel whenever practical.
* Not in conflict with authoritative framework documents.

Public availability alone does not establish official authority.

Authority shall be determined according to the established Authority Hierarchy.


---

## Supersession Principle

New publications may supersede earlier versions.

Superseded publications should remain preserved whenever practical.

Historical continuity is strengthened through preservation rather than deletion.

---

## Archival Principle

Historical publications should remain accessible whenever practical.

Archives provide evidence of framework evolution and support institutional continuity.

Preservation is preferred over removal.

---

## Publication Integrity

Published materials should remain consistent with:

* Constitutional Documents
* Charter Documents
* Governance Frameworks
* Official Definitions
* Established Authority Hierarchy

Publication should not contradict authoritative framework documents.

---

## Future Publication Channels

Future publication channels may include:

* Official Repository Releases
* Websites
* Documentation Portals
* Research Publications
* Educational Publications
* Articles
* Knowledge Bases
* Governance Case Studies

The choice of publication channel shall evolve according to stewardship priorities.

---

## Long-Term Objective

The long-term objective of publication is to preserve the knowledge, principles, governance, and historical evolution of SARGEN™ across generations.

Visibility is beneficial.

Continuity is essential.
