# SARGEN™ Glossary v1.0

## SARGEN™

System-Anchored, Rule-Led Governance & Evolving Network.

A governance and automation framework designed to create structured, accountable, and continuously evolving systems through predefined rules and governed execution.

---

## Governance

The system of rules, structures, responsibilities, and controls that guide how a system operates, evolves, and maintains accountability.

---

## Rule-Led

An operating approach where actions, decisions, and processes are guided primarily by predefined rules rather than individual discretion.

---

## System

A structured collection of people, processes, information, and resources working together toward defined objectives.

---

## Framework

An organized structure of principles, rules, methods, and components used to guide implementation and operation.

---

## Automation

The execution of defined processes through systems, tools, or mechanisms with reduced manual intervention while maintaining governance and accountability.

---

## Evolution

The controlled and continuous improvement of a system while preserving its foundational principles and structural integrity.

---

## Accountability

The condition in which actions, decisions, and outcomes can be traced to defined responsibilities within a governed system.

---

## Continuity

The ability of a system to maintain operation, governance, and purpose despite personnel, technology, or organizational changes.

---

## Execution

The implementation of defined actions, processes, and responsibilities according to established rules and governance structures.

---

## Creation Must Evolve

The foundational philosophy of SARGEN™ stating that systems, organizations, and processes should continuously improve through governed evolution rather than uncontrolled change.

---

## Structural Integrity

The preservation of a system's foundational principles, governance model, and intended purpose while allowing controlled adaptation and growth.

---

## Governance-Embedded System

A system in which governance is built into operational structures and processes rather than relying primarily on individual oversight.

---

## Long-Term Sustainability

The ability of a system to remain functional, accountable, maintainable, and relevant over extended periods of time.
